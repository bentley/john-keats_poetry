On Death

Can death be sleep, when life is but a dream, And scenes of bliss pass as a phantom by ?

The transient pleasures as a vision seem. And yet we think the greatest pain’s to die.

How strange it is that man on earth should roam. And lead a life of woe, but not forsake

His rugged path ; nor dare he view alone His future doom, which is but to awake.
